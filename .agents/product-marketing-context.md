## Product Overview

**One-liner:** AI-driven GTM engineering studio that adds 2–5 new logos to early-stage founders' pipeline every month.

**What it does:** Marketing Boutique is a small GTM engineer studio (Bay Area + India) that runs signal-based outbound campaigns and builds AI agents for growth/marketing/sales teams. We replace what a $200K in-house GTM engineer would do, productized into a $1K starter and a custom-quoted AI agent build.

**Product category:** GTM-as-a-service / outsourced GTM engineering. Sits on the "outbound + AI agents" shelf — not a traditional SDR agency, not a SaaS tool.

**Product type:** Service business (productized + custom). Two SKUs:
1. Outbound Campaign Starter (productized, $1K)
2. AI Agents for GTM/Growth/Marketing (custom quote)

Plus a free top-of-funnel offer (GTM audit + strategy call).

**Business model:** Project-based fees + custom AI agent retainers.

**Pricing:**
- Free GTM Audit + Strategy Call — $0 (top of funnel)
- Outbound Campaign Starter — $1,000 (down from $5,000, evergreen scarcity, 14-day delivery)
- AI Agents (Sales Intelligence, PMF, Company Research, Competitor Research, Churned Customer Reactivation) — custom quote: per-agent / bundle / retainer

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** "Our outbound stopped working and we can't get a GTM hire across the finish line."

**Specific manifestations (verbatim from ICP):**
1. Replies have dropped — generic SDR plays burned out
2. Agencies didn't deliver — too templated, no real personalization, vanity meetings
3. Can't hire a GTM Engineer in-house — too expensive ($180K–$220K) and tight market
4. Curious about AI in marketing/sales but stuck at prompt-level stuff
5. No bandwidth on existing team — founder is doing too much already

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- **Traditional SDR agencies:** Templated outreach, no signal stacking, optimize for meetings booked (not qualified), $5K–$15K/month minimums
- **Hiring in-house:** $200K all-in for a senior GTM Engineer, 3–6 month time-to-hire, hard to retain at early stage
- **Generic enrichment tools (Clay, Apollo solo):** Sells the hammer, not the nail. Founder still has to operate it.
- **Fractional CMOs / GTM advisors:** Strategy without execution. Slides, not pipeline.

**What it costs them:**
- Months of dead pipeline burning runway
- $50K–$100K of failed agency spend
- Founder hours that should be on product or fundraise
- Compounding deficit of warm relationships in their target market

**Emotional tension:**
- "I should know how to fix this — I'm the founder"
- Embarrassment about pipeline numbers in board updates
- Anxiety about hiring (right person? right time?)
- Skepticism about agencies after a previous burn
- FOMO on AI — "everyone's using it, we're behind"

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
1. **GTM Engineers, not SDRs** — we ship signal-stacked systems, not list-rentals
2. **Bay Area + India team** — quality + cost arbitrage means the $1K productized offer is sustainable for us and accessible for the customer
3. **Productized $1K starter** — most direct competitors start at $5K/month retainers; we de-risk the first engagement to a fixed-scope project
4. **AI agent dev capability** — most outbound agencies can't build the AI agents; most AI agencies don't ship outbound. We do both.
5. **Founder-led content as bonus** — $2,500 of free LinkedIn ghostwriting bundled into the $1K starter, so the customer's brand compounds while pipeline runs
6. **Signal-based, not template-based** — we read recent posts, hiring signals, fundraises, stack changes. Every DM has 2 specific insights.

**How we do it differently:**
- We build a **per-prospect cheat sheet** before sending the first DM (most agencies don't even look at the prospect's LinkedIn)
- We run **5 channels in parallel** (LinkedIn invite + DM + cold email + InMail + company page DM + post engagement) — most run 1–2
- We **qualify rejections** with a humor-led break-up that turns nos into market intel for the next wave
- We **wave-test** our messaging — Wave 1 validates, Wave 2 refines, Wave 3 closes — instead of blasting all leads on day 1

**Why that's better:**
- 35%+ acceptance rates vs. agency benchmarks of 15–20%
- Qualified meetings, not "discovery calls" with the wrong title
- Customer keeps the cheat sheets, the messaging, the AI agents — not locked in
- Pricing transparency at the entry point

**Why customers choose us:**
- "I've burned $50K on agencies. $1K to test you is a no-brainer."
- "You actually read my LinkedIn before pitching."
- "You're a team of GTM engineers, not SDRs reading a script."
- "You can also build the AI agent I've been Notion-ing about for 6 months."

## Brand Voice

**Tone:** Confident, direct, no-hedge. We talk to founders the way a senior GTM engineer would talk to a peer — not the way an agency talks to a client.

**Style:**
- Short sentences. Specific nouns. Active verbs.
- Lead with the insight, not with introductions
- Numbers > adjectives
- Lingo > marketing-speak ("pipeline," "logos," "signals" — not "growth opportunities")
- One question at a time
- Humor when warranted (see: $500 trillion break-up message)

**Personality (3–5 adjectives):**
1. **Operator** (we ship, we don't slide-deck)
2. **Specific** (we name the tool, the signal, the company)
3. **Confident** (we make the recommendation, we don't ask permission)
4. **Pragmatic** (we'll tell a prospect "wrong-time, come back in 6 months" if true)
5. **Slightly irreverent** (humor in break-ups, no startup-bro hedging)

**Sentence-level rules:**
- ❌ "Happy to chat / Hope this finds you well / Just wanted to check in"
- ✅ "Quick read on {{company}} — two angles I'd push on"
- ❌ "We help startups grow"
- ✅ "We add 2–5 new logos to pipeline every month"
- ❌ "— Pratik" sign-off on every DM
- ✅ Name in signature; body of message leads with the value
